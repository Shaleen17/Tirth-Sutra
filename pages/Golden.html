Golden Temple: Pool of Nectar

Welcome to Sri Harmandir Sahib, known to the world as the Golden Temple. This is more than a place of worship; it is a Tirtha of universal spirituality, a sanctuary of peace that opens its four doors to people of all faiths. To be here is to witness the core principles of Sanatana Dharma—selfless service (*seva*), community (*sangat*), and equality—in living practice.

The Sacred Origin: The Amrit Sarovar

The history of this sacred site is ancient. It is believed that the fourth Sikh Guru, Guru Ram Das, excavated the holy tank, which came to be known as *Amrit Sarovar* or the "Pool of Nectar." The water is revered for its healing properties. His successor, Guru Arjan Dev, designed and built the Harmandir Sahib in the center of this sacred pool. Uniquely, he designed it to be on a lower level than the surrounding land and with four entrances, symbolizing that all are welcome, regardless of caste, creed, or status.

Key Spiritual Experiences

Listening to Gurbani Kirtan: The soul of the Golden Temple is the continuous, melodious recitation of *Gurbani* (hymns from the Guru Granth Sahib). The divine vibrations of the kirtan resonate throughout the complex 24 hours a day, creating an atmosphere of profound peace and contemplation.

Partaking in the Langar: The Guru-ka-Langar (the Guru's kitchen) is a remarkable institution that embodies the principle of equality. Thousands of pilgrims, from the richest to the poorest, sit together on the floor and are served a simple, wholesome vegetarian meal by volunteers. Participating in this act of *seva*—either by serving or eating—is a deeply humbling and unifying experience.

The Palki Sahib Ceremony: Witnessing the Palki Sahib ceremony is a moving experience. Each night, the holy scripture, the Guru Granth Sahib, is carried in a gilded palanquin from the main shrine to its resting place. In the early morning, it is carried back. The procession is accompanied by hymns and fervent devotion.
