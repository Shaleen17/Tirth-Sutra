Dwarka: Kingdom of Krishna

Welcome to Dwarka, the legendary golden city of Lord Krishna, established on the western shores of Bharatavarsha. As one of the original four Char Dhams and one of the seven sacred Sapta Puris, Dwarka is a pilgrimage site of paramount importance. To visit this sacred land is to walk through the living history of the *Mahabharata* and the *Bhagavata Purana*, connecting with the divine majesty of Krishna as the King of Kings.

The Puranic Origin of Dwarka

After defeating Kansa, Lord Krishna led the Yadavas from Mathura to escape the repeated attacks of Jarasandha. He invoked Vishwakarma, the divine architect, to build a magnificent, fortified city on land reclaimed from the sea. The result was Dwarka, a city of gold, described in the scriptures as having grand palaces, wide roads, and divine gardens. It is believed that the ancient city was submerged by the sea after Lord Krishna's departure from this world, and the modern city stands as its inheritor.

Key Spiritual Experiences

Dwarkadhish Temple (Jagat Mandir): The heart of the pilgrimage is the magnificent Dwarkadhish Temple. Its five-story spire, supported by 72 pillars, is a marvel of Chalukya architecture. A key ritual here is witnessing the *dhvaja* (flag) being changed five times a day, a great honor for the families who sponsor it.

Gomti Ghat Snan: Taking a holy dip at the Gomti Ghat, where the Gomti River meets the sea, is a vital ritual for purifying the soul before seeking the *darśana* of Lord Dwarkadhish.

Bet Dwarka: A boat journey to the island of Bet Dwarka is essential. This is believed to be the actual residential palace of Lord Krishna and his queens. The island is dotted with temples and has an atmosphere of intimate devotion.

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga: Located near Dwarka, this is one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. A visit here is a powerful reminder of the unity between Shiva and Vishnu, a core tenet of Sanatana Dharma.
